Mary Barra’s Early Life and Education

Barra began her career with General Motors at the age of 18 as a co-op student in 1980. She held various engineering and administrative positions, gaining extensive experience and knowledge about the company and the automotive industry.

Leadership Roles at General Motors

Barra’s leadership journey at General Motors began in 2008 when she became the Vice President of Global Manufacturing Engineering. In 2011, she was appointed Senior Vice President of Global Product Development. Her responsibilities included design, engineering, program management, and quality of vehicles for General Motors worldwide.

In 2014, Barra made history by becoming the first female CEO of a major global automaker. Her appointment came at a challenging time for the company, which was grappling with a series of recalls due to safety issues. However, Barra’s decisive leadership helped steer the company through the crisis.
