What Are The Safest Neighborhoods In Seattle

Publish date: 2024-08-07
People are currently reading this guide.

So You Want to Live in Seattle? But Like, the Non-Murdery Part?

Ah, Seattle. The Emerald City. Land of grunge music, Pike Place Market, and...uh...crime? Don't worry, we all know Seattle's charm extends far beyond dodging stray bowling pins (looking at you, Fremont). But if you're new to the city and have a healthy aversion to being mugged by a particularly enthusiastic seagull, then this guide to Seattle's safest neighborhoods is for you!

Crime Can't Rain on Your Parade (Because it Probably Will Rain Anyway)

Let's be honest, Seattle isn't exactly Gotham City. But it does have its...interesting areas. So, how do we separate the charmingly eccentric from the places where even Batman would need a vacation? Here's the lowdown on Seattle's safest bets:

  • West Seattle - Alki Point and Fauntleroy take the cake (or should we say, the fresh-caught salmon?) on the west side. Stunning views, wealthy neighborhoods (which means fewer folks looking to "borrow" your wallet), and excellent schools make these areas a haven for families and safety seekers alike. Just don't get too relaxed – those seagulls are a menace!

  • The Eastside Escape - Feeling adventurous? Look across the pond (well, Puget Sound) to the suburbs of Kirkland and Redmond. While they may lack the edgy cool of downtown Seattle, they make up for it in safety, top-rated schools, and a strong sense of community. Plus, if you work for a certain tech giant based in Redmond, your commute just got a whole lot easier (and safer).

  • Hidden Gems - Don't discount the underdog! Neighborhoods like Hawthorne Hills and Broadmoor offer a surprising mix of affordability and safety. Plus, Hawthorne Hills boasts some of the best views in the city – perfect for sipping your morning coffee and pretending you don't secretly fear rogue pigeons.

Safety First, But Fun Never Rests

Sure, safety is important, but you also want a place with a life pulse, right? Here's the good news: most of Seattle's safe havens are also bursting with things to do. West Seattle has beach walks and charming shops galore. The Eastside offers family-friendly activities and a burgeoning craft brewery scene. And Hawthorne Hills? Well, they have a killer view (did we mention that already?).

FAQ: Your Guide to Seattle Survival (Minus the Danger)

  • How to avoid getting dive-bombed by a seagull? Easy – don't eat your lunch on the pier. Seriously, those birds are ruthless.
  • How to navigate the never-ending drizzle? Embrace it! Invest in a good raincoat and some waterproof boots. Bonus points for a sassy rain hat.
  • How to find the best cup of coffee? There's a good chance there's a stellar coffee shop within a block of wherever you are. Just follow the aroma of roasted beans and the faint murmur of existential poetry.
  • How to avoid rush hour traffic? Don't drive! Embrace Seattle's excellent public transportation system, or, if you're feeling fancy, hop on a ferry for a scenic commute.
  • How to fit in with the locals? Learn a little grunge history, complain about the rain (even if you secretly love it), and be prepared to discuss the merits of different coffee roasts at length.

So there you have it! With a little planning and a healthy dose of humor, you can find your own safe and vibrant corner of Seattle. Now get out there and explore – just maybe avoid that dark alley with the flickering neon sign that reads "Bargain Bagels." You never know what kind of deals they might be offering...
