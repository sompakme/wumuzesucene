Introduction to Eterneva and Its Journey on Shark Tank

Eterneva is an innovative company that caught the public’s eye with its unique business model: turning the ashes of loved ones, both people and pets, into diamonds. This concept not only offers a novel way to memorialize the deceased but also taps into the emotional and sentimental aspects of remembrance. The company’s appearance on the popular TV show “Shark Tank” further propelled its visibility and success. In this article, we will delve into Eterneva’s journey, its valuation on Shark Tank, and its estimated net worth in 2024.

The Founding of Eterneva

Eterneva was founded with a mission to change the way we perceive and deal with the loss of loved ones. The founders, Adelle Archer and Garrett Ozar, were inspired by personal experiences with loss and sought to create a meaningful way to honor the memory of the deceased. Their innovative approach to creating diamonds from ashes was not only a business venture but also a deeply personal mission.

Eterneva’s Business Model

The company’s business model is based on a multi-step process that involves high technology and personal customization. Customers send in a small amount of ashes, which are then subjected to high pressure and high temperature to simulate the natural diamond-forming conditions. The result is a unique diamond that can be customized in various cuts, colors, and settings, providing a lasting and personal memorial.

Breakthrough on Shark Tank

Eterneva’s appearance on “Shark Tank” was a pivotal moment for the company. The founders pitched their business to the Sharks, seeking an investment to scale their operations. Their unique business proposition, combined with a strong emotional appeal, intrigued the Sharks and garnered a significant amount of interest.

Investment and Valuation on Shark Tank

During their pitch, Eterneva requested a $600,000 investment in exchange for a 5% equity stake, which valued the company at $12 million. After negotiations, they secured an investment from Mark Cuban, who offered $600,000 for a 9% equity stake, effectively valuing the company at around $6.7 million post-deal.
