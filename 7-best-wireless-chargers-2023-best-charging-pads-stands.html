WHAT IS WIRELESS CHARGING?

While wireless charging wasn’t overly successful when it was first introduced, it has come a long way since.

Using magnetic induction, wireless chargers power up the battery of compatible devices through a charging pad or base station instead of through cables.

It may not beat out good old wired charging in terms of speed, but it’s convenient, can cut down on cable clutter and allows you to charge up multiple devices in one go.

Do wireless chargers work on all phones?

No, only certain phones have wireless charging capabilities. Most recent smartphone models do support wireless charging, but you should check the specifics of your model before investing in a wireless charger. Compatible phones are usually marked with “Qi wireless charging” or simply “wireless charging”.

You’ll also need to ensure that the wireless charger you’re interested in is compatible with your smartphone model.

Is wireless charging bad for your phone?

Any form of charging will wear your battery out over time, but wireless charging shouldn’t be any more harmful to your device than standard wired charging. While it is true that wireless charging generates more heat than wired charging, which can put more strain on the battery, both your charger and smartphone should be designed to manage this effectively.

To ensure heat doesn’t exceed safe limits, it’s a good idea to invest in a wireless charger from a reputable brand which includes safety features like temperature protection.

Can I charge my phone wirelessly if I have a case?

Most phone cases can be used with wireless charging, as long as they aren’t too thick. You can also buy specific phones cases, such as MagSafe-enabled cases for iPhone, to ensure they’ll work with a compatible charger.
