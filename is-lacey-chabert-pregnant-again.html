Lacey Chabert’s Career: From Early Roles to Film and Voice Work

Lacey Chabert embarked on her acting career at a young age, making her debut at just nine years old in the 1991 TV movie “A Little Piece of Heaven.” Her talent did not go unnoticed, earning her an award nomination.

In 1993, she portrayed Erica Kane’s daughter in the TV show “All My Children.” The following year, in 1994, she took on the role of Claudia, a teenager, in the drama series “Party of Five.”

The show centered around five siblings managing their parents’ restaurant after their passing and enjoyed a successful run from 1994 to 2000, earning awards for both the show and Chabert. Chabert ventured into voice acting, lending her voice to the character Eliza in the animated series “The Wild Thornberrys” from 1998 to 2004.

She also voiced Eliza in two movies and contributed her voice to other projects like “Balto 2: Wolf Quest” in 2002 and her portrayal of Gwen Stacy in the animated series “The Spectacular Spider-Man” in 2008-2009. During the nineties, she continued her voice work in TV shows and commercials, including “Hey Arnold!” and “Hercules.”

In 1997, Chabert sang for a young character in the Disney movie “Anastasia” and acted in the 1998 film “Lost in Space.” She also voiced Meg in the first season of “Family Guy” in 1999 before Mila Kunis took over the role.

Lacey Chabert’s Film and Voice Acting Career continued to thrive. In 2001, she played small roles in films such as “Tart” and “Not Another Teen Movie.” The year 2002 saw her in “A Scoundrel’s Wife,” followed by “Daddy’s Daycare” in 2003.

Her breakthrough came in 2004 when she portrayed Gretchen in “Mean Girls” alongside Lindsay Lohan, Rachel McAdams, and Tina Fey, earning them an MTV Movie Award.

Chabert appeared in various movies, including “Dirty Deeds” in 2005 and “Black Christmas” in 2006. Afterwards, she focused on smaller films and TV movies, such as “The Pleasure Drivers” and “Be My Baby.” In the 2010s, she starred in movies like “Anything is Possible” and “The Lost Tree.”

Apart from her notable role in “Mean Girls,” Chabert became famous for her involvement in numerous Hallmark movies, earning her the title “Queen of Hallmark.” She made guest appearances in TV shows like “The Ghost Whisperer” and “Teachers” and had longer roles in series like “Baby Daddy” and “Still the King.”

Additionally, Chabert ventured into voice roles for TV shows like “The Proud Family” and “Robot Chicken.” She voiced a character in “Bratz” and appeared in two “Bratz” movies. Her voice talents extended to series like “Kulipari” and “Young Justice.” She even lent her voice to characters in video games, including “The Wild Thornberrys: Animal Adventures” and “Sonic the Hedgehog.”
